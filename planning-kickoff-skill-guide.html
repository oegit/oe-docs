Claude Skill

Planning Skill:
install, configure, and use

A skill that turns Claude into your Planning strategist. Install it once, trigger it with a phrase, and use it in any Claude account.

What is a skill?

Think of a skill as a recipe Claude keeps and consults on its own. You don't open the recipe, copy it, or memorize it: when the conversation calls for it, Claude pulls it out of the drawer and follows it. This skill contains the complete project-Planning process, templates included.

Installation One time only

Download the skill planning-kickoff.skill

Upload the skill file

Download planning-kickoff.skill, then upload it via Settings → Features → Custom Skills → Upload (or drop it directly into a chat). Either way, it appears as a card with the skill's name.

Click "Save skill"

The button is on the card. That installs the skill in your account — available in all your new chats, on any device.

How to use it

No commands, no menus. It activates through normal conversation. Here's what a real session looks like:

New chat · Claude app
You Let's plan the redesign of my business website. 📎 specs.md · client-notes.pdf
Claude Great. Before building the plan, answer these questions in one batch: 1) What's the main goal of the redesign? 2) What's out of scope? 3) Is there a deadline? ...
You 1) Increase conversions. 2) We're not touching the blog. 3) End of August...
Claude Here's your phased plan: executive summary, assumptions to verify, ordered tasks each with a verification step, risks, and what was left out. 📄 PLAN_REDESIGN.md

The trigger phrase: "let's plan...", "help me plan...", "new project: ...". Claude detects the intent and loads the skill on its own.

Open a new chat

Pick the most capable model you have available — good Planning is worth the spend. Turn on extended thinking if your plan allows it.

Say what you want to plan and attach what you have

Documents, notes, briefs, links. Golden rule: the more real evidence you provide, the fewer assumptions the plan will contain.

Answer the questions

Claude will ask you at most ~6 questions, all at once, one time only. Answer them and wait.

Receive and review the plan

It arrives split into phases, with ordered tasks, risks, and a list of "assumptions to verify". Save it — it's your map for execution.

The skill's two modes

The skill carries two internal templates and picks the right one depending on where you're working.

Chat mode

  • For strategic Planning: campaigns, new projects, decisions.
  • Claude works only with what you attach.
  • Anything it can't confirm gets flagged "to verify" — never invented.

Claude Code mode

  • For projects that live in folders and code.
  • Claude reads your real files and confirms every assumption before planning.
  • It touches nothing without your explicit approval.

What you do NOT have to do

✕Open the templates. The skill's internal files are read by Claude, not by you.
✕Copy-paste prompts. A natural phrase ("let's plan...") is enough.
✕Reinstall it. Install once and it stays in your account.
✕Plan everything. Small, obvious tasks don't need a formal plan — just do them.

Quick questions

Does it work in any Claude account?

Yes. Anyone with the .skill file can install it in their own account with "Save skill". It doesn't depend on your organization or your plan.

How do I share it with someone else?

Send them the planning-kickoff.skill file (chat, email, or drive). They upload it via Settings → Features → Custom Skills (or drop it into a chat) and click "Save skill". Done.

What if it doesn't activate on its own?

Mention it directly: "use the planning-kickoff skill to plan this". That forces it every time.

When should I NOT use the skill?

When the task takes less than half an hour and the scope is obvious. Planning a 10-minute task is bureaucracy, not rigor.

Is the resulting plan final?

It's a plan with flagged assumptions. If the project lives in files or code, the next step is verifying those assumptions against reality (Claude Code mode) before executing.
